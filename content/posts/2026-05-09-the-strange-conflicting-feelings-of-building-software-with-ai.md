I wanted to write this post to talk about some of the weird and often conflicting feelings I have when I use AI to write software.

The first topic is about how there's this feeling of knowing that AI will write better code than I would have and probably ever could. I use Claude Code at work and on my personal projects and it genuinely impresses me nearly every time. It's architectural decisions and choice of patterns are _usually_ better than anything I could have made on my own. I still review everything it produces and occasionally make changes or ask it to improve something, but it's no different than reviewing another engineer's output and making comments. For the most part, however, I'm usually learning from _it_. Or, at least, realizing that it's been more thorough and less **lazy** than I would have.

On the other hand, there's still a level of stigma around using fully generated code and a vague feeling of, "I'm responsible for this but I also feel like I cheated." Like, I _made_ it... but did I? Does this count as something I built?

And then, I'm not going to stop using it. Not only is this exciting and full of hype, but it's genuinely a game-changer for how I work. 

I **love** code. I work in code, I hobby in code, and I think in code. I love the process of writing code and building software. Even so, I'm 100% onboard with AI. 

I still take time to manually write code, but it's more surgical than anything else. I use AI from start to finish for nearly everything and I sometimes feel the urge to _hide_ or _obscure_ that fact. It feels icky. But I'm also building more, faster, and at higher quality, regularly and without burnout.

At work and at home, I have started using additional tools and workflows, like using [Superset]() with `git worktree`s to maintain multiple streams of work on multiple different projects. Some apps are written from the start with AI and others were started with manual coding and have transitioned to AI. One of my apps, I hadn't actually touched since starting to use AI. My first task was asking Claude to do a full review and audit and to develop a step-by-step plan to address every issue and suggestion. It found _all_ of my bad patterns and tons of bugs - ones that I knew about and ones that I didn't - and I was able to implement everything in a couple evenings. My app is now **better** because of adopting AI; that means a better user experience and a more stable app in just a couple hours and at a level beyond my capability.

I'm a huge AI enthusiast and something of an evangelist, to the chagrin of my wife and friends. I don't like all the talk I hear about AI being terrible because it produces awful code that no one understands. If you know what you're doing and go in with a plan (even an AI-generated plan), you can have terrific results. 

At the same time, I'm tired of the AI hype and marketing (so much marketing) and firmly believe that **not everything needs AI** and they should stop forcing it on us at every opportunity.

Like I said, conflicting feelings.
